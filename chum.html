About Michael Chum Dew

Hello, I’m Michael Chum Dew, but I prefer to go by Chum. I’ve been a UI/UX designer for over 7 years. I currently work from home as a freelance designer living with my husband and daughter in Escondido, CA. I'm originally from Massachusetts. I’m 2nd-gen Cambodian American. I’m a fan of National Parks. My favorite color is green, and I enjoy crafting and up-cycling (Check out “Love” to view my side-projects).

I am confidently flawed with room to grow. I enjoy making mistakes on a daily basis. My thoughts are consistently inconsistent and tend to dwell on what others think. I speak up for others but let my designs do the talking. I’m a friend of failure, have great respect for process, and secretly admire change.

I enjoy working on small teams and thrive in an inclusive and creative environment.

If you are here to find out about why I like Comic-sans, here’s why:

  1. Throughout middle school I didn't like reading. I was enrolled into ESL (English as a second language) class. I didn’t know it at the time, I also have a learning disability and had issues with understanding and reading in general.

    At the end of the school year, the janitor cleared out unclaimed lockers and gave my brother and I comic books he found. I read those comics that summer and that was the first time I enjoyed and grasped what I read.
  2. Studies show Comic-sans can help people with dyslexia. Most stylized fonts have subtle discrepancies between serif and san-serif which can be difficult to understand for dyslexics. The handwritten look of Comic-sans provides familiarity that resembles their handwriting, making it easier to read for dyslexics.
